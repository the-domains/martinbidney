Professor Emeritus of English and Comparative Literature at Binghamton University (NY), Martin Bidney wrote two critical works–Blake and Goethe, then Patterns of Epiphany–before beginning his 11 books of translated and original verse. Violinist and vocalist, he writes melodious replies to each lyric he renders from German, Russian, or Polish in books of form-true “dialogic” poetry. In East-West Poetry and A Unifying Light his poems comment on verses of the Qur’an. In West-East Divan his poems reply to those of Goethe, in Poems of Wine and Tavern Romance to lyrics of the medieval Persian poet Hafiz. He has a book of lyrical “con-verse-ations” with the Crimean Sonnets of Mickiewicz. In Shakespair he responds to the Bard’s 154 sonnets with his own on facing pages.
